Life.


They have seven lives?!

The vast majority of cats live on average 11 to 12 years, however, it is important to note that this is an average, since this life time is directly related to the animal's lifestyle. An example would be the difference in the life span of a cat that lives inside the house, which can live up to 18 years (average), and another that lives in the street that normally reaches 8 years.
The belief in the seven lives of cats may have arisen when it was found that cats were able to withstand various prejudices, as well as accidents and mistreatment. As for the number seven, it would be because he is a kabbalist. Therefore, such a belief has originated because of the mood of mystery and magic that has always haunted the lives of cats throughout history.
Living Together.


A scratch does not hurt!

The cat has a very strong hunting instinct. Cats usually do not eat their "victims," preferring to play with them or show their owners. When it comes to your relationship with humans, there are two types of cats. The cat we can call "meek", who had contact people since childhood and learned to interact with our species without considering it a threat. They like to be around people, climb on their lap, receive long caresses. Another type of cat is one that does not allow himself to be approached by a human under any circumstances. These cats, called "ariscos", maintain a safe distance of our species, because throughout their childhood they were isolated of our conviviality. "Greedy" cats will very rarely get used to humans, even though they both live under one roof for many years.
A typical dog thing is to obey. A typical cat thing is not obeying. The cat simply does not think it has to do what it is not in order and is not even "rebellious" because it does not perceive what you want. You call the cat into the house, for example, and if he's doing something interesting, he will not come. Even so, it is possible to induce the cat to do what you want in a certain circumstance, but not through an order.
